Lamp

A sleek dimable LED desk light for for Instead of buying a lamp for my desk, I decided to design my own.

While the end product may look simple, this lamp was the result of a lengthy design process with many learning opportunities along the way.

I entered this project with a clear vision of how I wanted the lamp to look and as a result, most of the design work was spent trying to retrofit the electronics into a "visual shell". One particular challenge was mounting the potentiometer dimmer switch. The tight space requirements demanded a creative solution designed entirely around the potentiometer itself in addition to some "abrasive" modifications to the potentiometer housing.

I also applied rapid prototyping skills to perfect the hinge mechanism in order to satisfy specific design criteria. In addition to being cheap and easy to manufacture, the joints needed to be quickly adjustable via the knobs, strong enough to support the lamp when tightened, and allow the LED power wires to pass through and bend with the join.

When it came to fabrication, I used the manual mill, lathe, and SLA resin printer to make all of the custom parts. This project also gave me an opportunity to experiment with various finishing techniques including sand-blasting, powder-coating, and polishing.
