Mechanical Hopper

In the first year "hopper preject" at Olin College of Engineering, students build a jumping contraption inspired by nature and refined thorugh rapid prototpying. My design relies on a set three-bar-linkages to push the hopper off the ground using the potential energy stored in the "tendons" of the three legs. A spring-loaded claw and suction cup form the "trigger mechaism", allowing the machine to sponetatnously deploys the legs a few seconds after it is loaded. In addition to complying with the project's short list of approved material, I designed my hopper under the additonal constrain of wanting it to landing upright after each jump.

The final prototype met many of my design goals. I am most pleased with my unique tigger mechasism which was able to deploy the legs accuratly and consistently, leading to a controlled jump and landing.

To achive my final prototype, I followed a detailed design and prototypiting prcoess. Starting with simple concept sketches for various hopper designs, I moved on to building simple 3D sketch models to validate design choises (such as the trigger mechanism concept). While my first full prototype was already capable of a self-triggered jump, additional itterations were developed to refine the landing behavior.
