Eccentric Tensioner

Our current car uses a chaindrive to deliver power from the motor to the rear differential. The passive chain tensioner is a critcal componenet in the drietrain to ensure proper power delivery and eliminate shock loading in the chain. The tensioner relies on an off-center (eccentric) bearing block to make fine adjustments to the distance between the differentil and motor.

I used hand calculations to justify the bolt pattern with a . I went on to run a frictional contact study in Ansys mechanical to gain more insigt into the stength of the bolted connection. The results of the FEA sugest that the two bolts that I planned to use will sufice.

Once designed, I machined the parts on a CNC mill. I had to perform a few additional operations on the manual mill and lathe such as precion-boring out the bearing hole.

Our goal was to upgrate the steeting system to use a rack-and-pinion and this involved desigining a custome mountich solutoin. This project was a forya into using hand-calcultion and FEM simulations to justify a design. I went on to fabricate the brackets and install the new steering system on the vehicle.
